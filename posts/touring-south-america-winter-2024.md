# Touring South America - Winter 2024

* cyrsullivan
* Feb 5, 2025
* 2 min read

Updated: Oct 2, 2025

Following a hectic three weeks in Ottawa managing personal affairs and reconnecting with friends and family, including a Cyr Family Christmas in Cobourg, we set off for South America. Our one-month adventure in South America kicked off with a few days in Santiago, then we took a two-week cruise from Santiago to Buenos Aires, sailing around Cape Horn. From Buenos Aires, we flew south to Patagonia, and spent a week hiking in the Andes. Following a short return to Buenos Aires, we jetted off to to Sydney, Australia, Whew!

![](images/touring-south-america-winter-2024/image_0.jpg)

While in Santiago, we stayed near the Plaza de Armas de Santiago, located in the heart of the city. This area is a web of pedestrian malls and is home to buildings of historical and architectural significance. It is also a bustle of street vendors and buskers.

![](images/touring-south-america-winter-2024/image_1.jpg)

With extended daylight hours and short nights, Christmas lights are conspicuously absent. This shop window displays its seasonal support with an adorable Santiago skyline.

![](images/touring-south-america-winter-2024/image_2.jpg)

The entrance to Cerro Santa Lucía on Santa Lucía Hill features a charming small park with remnants of forts, an astronomical observatory, and public promenades that are centuries old. It is believed to be the location of an Inca settlement.

We wrapped up our one-month South American tour with a few days in the remarkable city of Buenos Aires. We explored various central neighbourhoods, absorbing the incredible sights and sounds. An unexpected delight was a boat tour of the Tigre Delta, a vibrant city with tranquil canals where boats are the sole means of transportation. Next destination, Australia!
