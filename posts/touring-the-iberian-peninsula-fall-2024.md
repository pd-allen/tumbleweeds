# Touring the Iberian Peninsula - Fall 2024

* cyrsullivan
* Dec 23, 2024
* 3 min read

After an emotional goodbye, we parted ways from our friends Paul and Rachel. While they returned to Maastricht, Sandy and I flew to Lisbon. There, we collected our rental car for a five-week trip around the Iberian Peninsula. Our journey took us north to Porto, east through the Peso da Régua wine region, then southeast through Coimbra, Évora, and Sevilla to Gibraltar, the entrance to the Mediterranean. After exploring the Rock, we continued west along the Algarve through Albufeira and Lagos, then headed back north. In Lisbon we returned the rental car and took a few days to relax before heading back to the whirlwind of Ottawa for a brief stop before setting out again.

Porto is a stunning and pedestrian-friendly city, full of vitality. It featured captivating architecture, walls adorned with 'azulejos' (hand-painted ceramic tiles), and narrow back alleys rich in local culture. We enjoyed exploring this particular alley decorated with fading bunting, likely remnants from a previous festival.

Seville was an absolute delight. Although it was once a walled city, only a small portion of the wall still stands. What remains is a collection of architecturally significant buildings reflecting Seville's rich history, including many from its Moorish period and numerous small religious chapels often concealed behind modest doors. The Plaza de España and the Cathedral de Sevilla are two especially noteworthy landmarks. We spent three days exploring the city centre and eagerly anticipate returning to continue our exploration.

The Rock proved to be an unforeseen surprize. It is essentially a fortress, featuring an extensive network of tunnels and gun emplacements that stretch for tens of miles. You can take an in-depth self-guided tour of all the sites in the Gibraltar Nature Reserve. Tickets available at: [https://naturereserve.gi/tickets/#](https://naturereserve.gi/tickets/)

The charm of the Algarve is found in its beautiful, rugged coastline and the vast network of trails stretching from Faro to Lagos and further. The scenery is spectacular, and the hiking can be as demanding as one wishes. The region around Praia da Rocha is especially stunning. This was our second visit, and I suspect it will not be our last.
